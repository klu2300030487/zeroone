  • Chicken Biryani

    Chicken Biryani is a traditional Indian rice dish renowned for its aromatic flavors and tender chicken pieces. It is a popular dish served across India and various other parts of the world, loved for its rich taste and fragrant spices. The dish typically consists of long-grain Basmati rice, marinated chicken, and a blend of spices such as cardamom, cloves, cinnamon, and bay leaves. To prepare Chicken Biryani, the chicken is first marinated in a mixture of yogurt and spices, which helps to tenderize the meat and infuse it with flavor. The rice is parboiled separately with whole spices until it's partially cooked. Then, layers of rice and chicken are assembled in a heavy-bottomed pot or a biryani pot, along with fried onions, fresh herbs like mint and cilantro, and sometimes saffron-infused milk for added aroma and color.

  • Mixed Biryani

    Mixed Biryani is a culinary masterpiece that combines the best of various worlds, blending the flavors of chicken, mutton, and prawns into a single, irresistible dish. Each bite is a symphony of tastes and textures, with tender mutton pieces, succulent prawns, and juicy chicken mingling with fragrant Basmati rice and aromatic spices. This indulgent dish begins with marinating the meats in a blend of spices and yogurt, infusing them with layers of flavor. The rice is parboiled with whole spices until partially cooked, ready to absorb the rich essence of the meats and spices. Layer by layer, the meats and rice are assembled in a pot, interspersed with caramelized onions, fresh herbs like mint and cilantro, and sometimes saffron-infused milk for added richness and color. The pot is then sealed and cooked using the traditional "dum" method, allowing the flavors to meld together beautifully.
