Dal Makhani

Dal Makhani is known for its rich and creamy texture and taste and is often served with flavored rice. Dal Makhana, also known as Makhani Dal or Makhani Moong Dal. Dal Makhani is a classic Noth Indian dish made with whole urad dal,rajma,butter and spices. The urad dal and lotus seeds are soaked and cooked until tender. Meanwhile, a rich tomato-onion gravy is prepared by sautéing onions, ginger, and garlic, then adding tomatoes and spices. Dal Makhana is often served hot, garnished with fresh cream and chopped cilantro.

Vegetable Biryani

This delicious layered biryani is fragrant, flavorful, and filling. Carrot, beans, peas and potatoes cooked in gravy of spicy yogurt and served with basmati rice topped with cashews and fried onions. To prepare vegetable biryani, the vegetables are usually sautéed with the spices to bring out their flavors. Basmati rice is soaked and partially cooked separately. Then, the rice and vegetable mixture are layered in a pot, with each layer seasoned and drizzled with ghee or oil for richness. Vegetable biryani is often served with raita along with papadums or naan bread.

Paneer Biryani

Paneer biryani is a delicious Indian dish made with paneer (Indian cottage cheese), fragrant spices, and long-grain basmati rice. It's a popular vegetarian version of the traditional biryani. Start by marinating the paneer cubes in a mixture of yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, biryani masala, and a pinch of salt for about 30 minutes. Then, shallow fry or pan-sear the marinated paneer until golden brown and set it aside. Rinse and soak basmati rice for 30 minutes. Add the soaked rice and cook until it's 70-80% done. Drain the rice and set it aside.

Rs.200

Mushroom Curry

Mushroom curry is a delicious and versatile dish that can be made in various styles, from creamy and mild to spicy and tangy. Mushroom curry is a delicious and flavorful dish that can be enjoyed with rice, naan, or roti. Clean the mushrooms thoroughly and slice them into medium-sized pieces. If using larger mushrooms, you can quarter or halve them as desired. Add the sliced mushrooms to the pan and mix well with the tomato-onion masala. Cook for a few minutes until the mushrooms start to release their moisture.

Chana Masala

Chana masala, also known as chole, is a popular North Indian dish made with chickpeas cooked in a flavorful and aromatic gravy. Heat oil in a pan and sauté finely chopped onions until they turn golden brown. Add minced ginger, garlic, and slit green chilies. Sauté until the raw aroma disappears and the onions are caramelized. Add the powdered spices - cumin powder, coriander powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, and red chili powder. Stir well to coat the onions evenly with the spices. This step enhances the flavor profile of the dish.

Aloo Gobi

Aloo Gobi is a classic Indian dish made with potatoes (aloo) and cauliflower (gobi), cooked in a flavorful blend of spices. Add ginger-garlic paste to the pan. Sauté until the raw smell disappears. Add turmeric powder, cumin powder, coriander powder, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well to coat the onions evenly with the spices. Add the diced potatoes to the pan. Cook for a few minutes until they start to soften.Serve hot with chapati, naan, or steamed rice. It pairs well with yogurt-based raita as a side dish

Bhindi Fry

Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. Add the sliced bhindi to the pan. Sauté them gently to coat them evenly with the oil. Sprinkle turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder,and salt over the bhindi. Mix well to ensure the spices coat the bhindi evenly. Continue cooking the bhindi on medium heat.Cook until the bhindi is almost tender and slightly crispy. This usually takes about 10-15 minutes. Bhindi Fry is typically served hot as a side dish with Indian bread like roti, paratha, or naan.

Rs.40

Paneer Butter Masala

Paneer Butter Masala is a popular and indulgent North Indian dish known for its rich and creamy gravy. Cut the paneer into cubes and shallow fry or pan-sear them until golden brown. Set aside the fried paneer cubes. In a pan, heat some butter and add chopped onions, ginger, and garlic. Sauté until the onions turn golden brown. Add chopped tomatoes and cashews to the onions. Cook until the tomatoes are soft and the cashews. It's comforting dish that's perfect for special occasions or when you want to indulge in a creamy, flavorful curry.
